How Data Breaches Work

The Journey of a Leaked Password

Modern hacking isn't a person typing at a terminal; it's an automated industry. Your data is usually stolen in bulk during a massive breach of a major service provider.

The Lifecycle of a Leak

01. Theft

A server vulnerability allows hackers to download a database containing millions of user records, including email addresses and passwords.

02. Decrypt

If the passwords aren't properly "salted," hackers use specialized hardware to crack the encryption, turning scrambled text back into readable passwords.

03. Exploit

Bots automatically test these email/password pairs on other sites like PayPal, Netflix, and Amazon. This is known as Credential Stuffing.

The Password Reuse Risk

Hackers rely on the fact that 65% of people reuse the same password across multiple platforms. One leak at a small gaming forum could give a hacker the key to your primary email or bank account.

Important: Strength doesn't save a reused password. Even a 20-character password is useless if it has been leaked in a plaintext database from a previous breach.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Never Reuse: Use our Generator for every new account.
  • Use MFA/2FA: Multi-factor authentication stops hackers even if they have your password.
  • Check Your Email: Use monitoring services to see if your data has been leaked.
