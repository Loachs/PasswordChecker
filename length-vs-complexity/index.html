Length vs. Complexity

The Science of Password Entropy

For years, we were taught that a "strong" password had to be short and full of complex symbols. Modern math proves that length is far more powerful than character variety.

The Entropy Equation

Password strength is measured in "bits of entropy." As you can see in the formula below, adding a single character to the length (L) has an exponential impact compared to just adding more character types (R).

The Formula
E = L × log₂(R)
• E = Total Entropy (Strength)
• L = Length of Password
• R = Number of possible characters

Brute Force Reality

Hackers use GPUs that can test billions of combinations per second. Here is how different styles of passwords hold up against a modern brute-force attack:

Type Example Length Crack Time
Short/Complex 4k#L!v9 7 Instant
Human Pattern Summer2024! 11 < 1 Day
Long Passphrase blue-elephant-2026 18 ~40,000 Years

Why Passphrases Win

A "Passphrase" uses 4-5 random words. They are incredibly long (high entropy) but much easier for the human brain to remember than a string of random gibberish like j%9!Lp#2.
